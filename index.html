About Me

I am a computational biologist specialized in mathematical modeling. I will be working as a scientist at Takeda, developing quantitatative systems pharmacology models for rare diseases. Before, I was a postdoctoral researcher in the Weinberger lab at the Gladstone Institutes of Virology and UCSF. I received my PhD in chemistry from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill mentored by Timothy Elston and Klaus Hahn.
