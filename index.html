About Me

I'm currently a postdoctoral scholar in Leor Weinberger's lab at the Gladstone Institute/UCSF, studying viruses with a combination of computational and experimental methods.

I got my PhD from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill mentored by Timothy Elston and Klaus Hahn developing computational tools to model and analyze biological signaling at the single-molecule level. I also frequently collaborated with our nearby neighbor Daniel Lew at Duke University. Before then, I received my BS at Northeastern University, where I first started doing research with Sunny Zhou on protein post-translational modifications, and with many folks at Amgen including Dawn Cohen and Matt Potter in the Small Molecule Process and Research Development division.
