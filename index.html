About Me

I am a computational biologist specialized in mathematical modeling. I work as a scientist at Takeda, developing quantitatative systems pharmacology models for rare disease research. Before, I was a postdoctoral researcher working on human cytomegalovirus and SARS-CoV-2 in the Weinberger lab at the Gladstone Institutes of Virology and UCSF. I received my PhD in chemistry from the University of North Carolina at Chapel Hill, studying the spatial and temporal coordination of signaling networks at the single-molecule level, mentored by Timothy Elston and Klaus Hahn. I obtained a BS in chemistry from Northeastern University, where I did protein chemistry research with Zhaohui Sunny Zhou and did analytical chemistry internships with Amgen's Small Molecule Process and Product Development team (including Dawn Cohen and Matthew Potter).
